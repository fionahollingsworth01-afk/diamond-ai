Quiet.

Psychological Symbolism:
Grave represents Jake's belief that steady determination outlasts panic. Neither horse nor rider seeks attention. Both simply endure until the job is finished.

Narrative Profile:
Jake Kincaid rode Grave the way a man rides when noise and nerves are liabilities. The dark bay Mustang moved beneath him without comment or fuss, head low, eyes forward, hooves placing themselves as if they already knew the ground's intentions. Jake didn't ride tight. He didn't need to. Grave didn't jump at wind in the grass or shadows cutting across the trail. He just kept going, quiet and deliberate, carrying Jake through rough country as if distance were something to be worn down, not fought.
When the land turned ugly, Jake trusted Grave to do the thinking that mattered. Loose rock, sudden drops, ground that looked solid until it wasn't—Grave sorted it without breaking rhythm. Jake stayed centered and still, letting the Mustang choose footing while he watched the horizon. There was a relentlessness to the horse, not speed for show, not flash, just forward motion that didn't quit.
Gunfire, shouting, confusion—it never changed Grave's breathing or his line. Jake chose him because fear never took the reins.
