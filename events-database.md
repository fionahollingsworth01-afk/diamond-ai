# FIVE OAKS EVENTS DATABASE

This file is a canon reference for major events in the Five Oaks saga. It is meant to help Diamond answer questions about what happened, who was involved, and why the event mattered.

## The Founders Meet and Begin Working Together

**Era:** Early 1880s.

**People:** Jake Kincaid, Krys Callahan, Matt Haskins, Luke Rawlins, Jace Callahan.

**What Happens:** Their paths come together through work, survival, family conflict, and shared danger.

**Why It Matters:** This is the beginning of the group that later becomes the five equal founders of Five Oaks.

---

## Johnson Farm Exploitation

**People:** Albert "Old Man" Johnson, Jake, Matt, Luke, Jace, Duncan.

**What Happens:** Johnson works the men hard, pays poorly, and treats them as disposable labor.

**Why It Matters:** His treatment helps convince them they need another way to survive.

---

## The Milliron Period

**People:** Henry and Martha Milliron, Jake, Matt, Luke, Krys, Ezra Pennington.

**What Happens:** The Millirons provide honest work and shelter. Ezra Pennington tries to gain access rights across their land.

**Why It Matters:** The founders learn what fair treatment looks like and recognize how land can be taken piece by piece through business pressure.

---

## Matt and Clara's Failed Store Robbery

**People:** Matt Haskins, Clara Sloan.

**What Happens:** At sixteen, Matt and Clara plan a robbery to gain enough money to marry. Matt intends to frighten the store owner, not kill anyone. The plan fails and Clara is shot.

**Outcome:** Clara dies in Matt's arms.

**Why It Matters:** Matt forms the belief that love creates risk, failed planning costs blood, and he is unsafe to love.

---

## Jake Leaves Victor's House

**People:** Jake, Victor, Curtis, Dwayne, Albert, Jake's mother.

**What Happens:** At thirteen, Jake leaves the abusive household.

**Why It Matters:** Jake chooses hunger over fear and spends the rest of his life building a home that will never resemble Victor's.

---

## Luke Leaves Home

**People:** Luke, Astrid, Iris, their parents.

**What Happens:** At fourteen, after years of drawing his father's violence toward himself to protect his mother and sisters, Luke leaves.

**Why It Matters:** Luke learns to use charm and humor as protection but carries guilt over leaving Astrid and Iris behind.

---

## The Bank Robbery

**Era:** 1884–1885.

**People:** Jake, Krys, Matt, Luke; Jace is absent.

**What Happens:** Krys works inside the bank and opens the rear exit. Matt plans. Jake remains outside. Luke is wounded with a knife and later develops a fever.

**Why It Matters:** The event tests the founders' loyalty and establishes Krys as an active inside participant rather than someone merely attached to the men.

---

## Founding Five Oaks

**People:** Jake, Krys, Matt, Luke, Jace.

**What Happens:** The five build a ranch and agree to equal ownership.

**Why It Matters:** Five Oaks becomes a chosen family instead of a traditional household ruled by one man.

---

## The Silas Vance Land Conflict

**People:** Silas Vance, Amos Bennett, Coy Walker, Dylan Kennedy, the founders.

**What Happens:** Vance tries to gain the creek property through pressure, agents, threats, and hired violence. Dylan Kennedy is hired to kill Krys.

**Outcome:** Krys kills Dylan Kennedy with an extraordinary long-range shot.

**Why It Matters:** The conflict protects Five Oaks but creates the legend surrounding Krys.

---

## Sheriff Baker Tests Krys

**People:** Krys, Sheriff Baker, deputy.

**What Happens:** Sheriff Baker doubts that Krys made the shot that killed Dylan Kennedy and makes her shoot bottles at increasing distances.

**Outcome:** Krys proves her skill.

**Why It Matters:** The demonstration spreads her reputation further.

---

## Paul Smith Comes for Krys

**People:** Paul Smith, Krys, Jake, Five Oaks family.

**What Happens:** Gunfighter Paul Smith comes to Red Willow Crossing after hearing about Krys. He wants to defeat her and strengthen his own legend.

**Why It Matters:** Krys wants no part of fame, while Jake is forced to face how terrified he is of losing her.

---

## Jake and Krys Marry

**People:** Jake, Krys, justice of the peace, Five Oaks family.

**Location:** Five Oaks.

**Why It Matters:** Krys chooses Jake over the respectable futures Roger preferred for her, including Oscar Dunbar and the possibility represented by Kael Flanagan.

---

## Kai Receives His Name

**People:** Krys, Jake, the traveling Indigenous man Kai.

**What Happens:** Krys meets Kai and receives permission to give his name to her unborn child. Jake resists at first but later agrees.

**Outcome:** Kai Weston Kincaid is named.

**Why It Matters:** The event reinforces Krys's right to choose and begins the next generation.

---

## Roger's Month at Five Oaks

**People:** Roger, Marie, Jace, Krys, Rhys, founders and family.

**What Happens:** Roger criticizes the ranch's equal ownership, communal meals, Luke and Emma cooking, Krys's closeness with Matt, and the name Kai. He humiliates Rhys and strikes him.

**Outcome:** Jace later steps in when Roger appears ready to strike Rhys again.

**Why It Matters:** Roger sees that Five Oaks runs according to values he cannot control.

---

## Roger's Final Conflict With Rhys

**People:** Roger, Rhys.

**What Happens:** After an argument, Rhys leaves the next morning and brushes past Roger when Roger tries to speak.

**Outcome:** Roger later dies from a massive heart attack at work. His last word to Rhys is Rhys's name.

**Why It Matters:** Rhys carries guilt over the unfinished conversation.

---

## Royce Seeks Refuge at Five Oaks

**People:** Royce and the founders.

**What Happens:** After a bank robbery kills most of his outlaw gang, Royce comes to Five Oaks for shelter.

**Outcome:** When he learns the law is asking questions, he leaves rather than bring danger to the ranch. He is later captured by a United States Territorial Marshal in Oklahoma and spends twenty-five years in prison.

**Why It Matters:** Royce proves that he still considers Five Oaks home even while believing he has no right to stay.

---

## Eli Leaves Home

**People:** Eli, Jace, Whisper Winston.

**What Happens:** At fifteen, Eli's anger toward Jace makes him vulnerable to Whisper's manipulation.

**Outcome:** Eli enters outlaw life for more than five years.

**Why It Matters:** Eli mistakes control for acceptance and spends years trying to protect his family from inside the gang.

---

## Eli Warns Five Oaks

**People:** Eli, Edgar Post, Whisper's men, Five Oaks family.

**What Happens:** Eli secretly sends a warning before Edgar Post's attack.

**Outcome:** Five Oaks prepares and survives. Edgar and most of his men are killed, wounded, or arrested.

**Why It Matters:** Eli's warning saves lives and proves that he never stopped protecting his family.

---

## Eli Leaves Ironjaw's Camp

**People:** Eli, James "Ironjaw" Winston.

**What Happens:** Eli leaves Ironjaw's hideout and rides for six days toward Missouri.

**Why It Matters:** It is the beginning of Eli's return home.

---

## Eli Watches Five Oaks From the Ridge

**People:** Eli and the Five Oaks family below.

**What Happens:** Eli watches family life from a distance but is afraid to step onto the ranch.

**Why It Matters:** The ridge represents his longing to belong and his belief that he must first make the family safe.

---

## Krys Is Shot

**People:** Krys, Eli, Matt, Jesse "Whisper" Winston.

**What Happens:** Whisper aims at Eli. Krys runs across the street and pushes Eli aside.

**Outcome:** Whisper shoots Krys. Eli kills Whisper moments later. Matt catches Krys and calls for the doctor.

**Why It Matters:** Krys proves that Eli is still her family. Eli is forced to act against Whisper and finally breaks free of him.

---

## Krys's Hospital Recovery

**People:** Krys, Jake, Matt, Luke, Jace, Kai, Wes, Sami, Eli, doctor.

**What Happens:** Krys undergoes two surgeries and remains unconscious for ten days. Jake practically lives at the hospital. Matt struggles with a severe episode because the shooting echoes Clara's death.

**Outcome:** Krys wakes and later fully recovers.

**Why It Matters:** The family crisis exposes how much every person depends upon her.

---

## Eli Returns to the Family

**People:** Eli, Jace, Susanna, Krys, Jake, Five Oaks family.

**What Happens:** Eli remains after Whisper's death and begins facing what happened between him and Jace.

**Outcome:** He eventually receives his own cabin.

**Why It Matters:** The cabin proves that the family expects him to stay.

---

## Matt Chooses Jennifer as His Heir

**People:** Matt, Krys, Jennifer.

**What Happens:** Matt tells Krys he wants Jennifer to inherit his full share of Five Oaks.

**Why It Matters:** Jennifer is the daughter Matt never had. She understands him, cares for him, and represents the person he trusts to protect his part of the ranch.

---

## Cole Meets Tate

**People:** Cole, Tate.

**What Happens:** Angry over Eli receiving a cabin, Cole rides to town and enters the saloon. He meets Tate at the door and later sits beside him.

**Why It Matters:** The encounter begins Cole's struggle between honest love and fear of losing his family.

---

## Kai Meets Paloma

**People:** Kai, Paloma, Isabella, Echevarría family.

**What Happens:** Kai sees Paloma in Red Willow Crossing and becomes immediately captivated.

**Why It Matters:** Paloma gives Kai the future he had stopped expecting.

---

## Kincaid and Echevarría Families Dine Together

**People:** Kai, Paloma, Mateo, Catalina, Isabella, Jake, Krys, Wes, Sami.

**Location:** The Belle Époque.

**What Happens:** The families meet over dinner. Mateo studies Kai while Catalina and Isabella connect warmly with the Kincaids.

**Why It Matters:** The two families begin joining before Kai and Paloma's wedding.

---

## Kai and Paloma Marry

**Location:** Five Oaks.

**People:** Kai, Paloma, Kincaid and Echevarría families.

**What Happens:** Paloma wears a lace mantilla Catalina brought from Spain.

**Why It Matters:** Their marriage expands Five Oaks without erasing Paloma's heritage.

---

## Wes and Sami Expect a Child

**People:** Wes, Sami, Jake, Krys, Kai, Luke, Emma.

**What Happens:** Wes and Sami invite the family to dinner and reveal that Sami is expecting.

**Outcome:** Wesley Lucas Kincaid becomes the next generation.

---

## Jennifer Chooses Waya and Tsula

**People:** Jennifer, Waya, Tsula, Jace, Susanna, Five Oaks family.

**What Happens:** Jennifer falls in love with Waya and accepts Tsula as part of that choice.

**Why It Matters:** Jennifer refuses to let prejudice define who belongs in her family.

---

## Five Oaks Defends Jennifer's Family

**People:** Jake, Jace, Luke, Matt, Jennifer, Waya, Tsula.

**What Happens:** The founders make clear that disrespect toward Jennifer, Waya, or Tsula will have consequences, including the possibility of moving Five Oaks business elsewhere.

**Why It Matters:** Private acceptance becomes public protection.

---

## Jace Accepts Tate

**People:** Jace, Cole, Tate, Susanna, Sawyer, Dawson.

**What Happens:** Jace works through his anger, confusion, and fear and accepts Tate as Cole's partner and part of the family.

**Outcome:** Sawyer and Dawson also accept Tate and speak to him normally as family.

**Why It Matters:** Five Oaks proves that protecting its own includes allowing them to live honestly.

---

## Sawyer and Dawson Present the Oil Plan

**People:** Sawyer, Dawson, Jake, Krys, Matt, Luke, Jace, Tate, Waya.

**What Happens:** The twins ask for land and twelve thousand dollars in financial backing.

**Positions:**
- Krys supports them.
- Jake is reluctant but ultimately stands with Krys.
- Matt worries about the money.
- Luke thinks they deserve a chance.
- Jace opposes the plan.
- Waya contributes knowledge.
- Tate surprises the founders with enough understanding to help persuade Matt.

**Outcome:** Jace is outvoted and the oil venture moves forward.

**Why It Matters:** The founders allow the next generation to take its own risk.

---

## Micah Hides in the Loft

**People:** Micah Ward, Krys.

**What Happens:** Fourteen-year-old Micah flees Fred's abuse and sleeps in the Five Oaks loft for days.

**Outcome:** Krys finds him bruised, hungry, and terrified she will send him back.

**Why It Matters:** Krys sees a child who needs help, not a trespasser.

---

## Krys Takes Micah In

**People:** Micah, Krys, Jake, Matt, Luke, Eli.

**What Happens:** Krys cleans Micah's injuries, feeds him, and gives him the couch in her and Jake's cabin because Cole and Tate still occupy the loft.

**Connections:**
- Jake sees the abused boy he once was.
- Luke sees himself at fourteen after fleeing home.
- Matt recognizes fear, hunger, and the habit of expecting disaster.
- Eli understands feeling unwanted and controlled.

**Why It Matters:** Five Oaks becomes the place its founders once needed themselves.

---

## Fred Leaves Micah Behind

**People:** Fred, David, Micah, Colin, Krys.

**What Happens:** Fred leaves with David and Colin and drives away while Micah stands watching with tears streaming down his face.

**Outcome:** Krys comes out to him after the truck disappears.

**Why It Matters:** Micah feels abandoned by his birth family, but Krys makes clear that he has not been left without a home.

---

## Marie Marries Bob Masters

**People:** Marie, Bob, Callahan family.

**What Happens:** Years after Roger's death, Marie marries Bob Masters.

**Why It Matters:** Bob gives Marie a later marriage built on patience, respect, and peace.

---

## The Founders' Inheritance Plan

**What Happens:** Each founder's share is directed toward the next generation.

**Plan:**
- Krys to Kai.
- Jake to Wes.
- Luke to Sami.
- Matt to Jennifer.
- Jace divided among Eli, Cole, Sawyer, Dawson, and Caleb.

**Why It Matters:** The land passes forward according to relationship, trust, and responsibility rather than simple birth order.
