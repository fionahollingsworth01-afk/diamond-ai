Rachel Williams
Role: Matt Haskins' mother
Family:
Mother of Matt Haskins.
Never married Matt's biological father.
Gave Matt the surname Haskins after his biological father despite raising him alone.
Core Spine:
Rachel loved her son in the moments addiction loosened its grip, but those moments became increasingly rare. Matt grew up never knowing which version of his mother he would find.
Background:
Rachel Williams became pregnant by a man named Haskins. When he learned she was expecting, he abandoned her before Matt was born. Rachel's own parents responded by throwing her out of their home. She spent much of her pregnancy living on the streets until, shortly before Matt's birth, she found a dilapidated, abandoned house and began squatting there. It became the only home Matt ever knew as a child, and it was in that same house that Rachel eventually died.
Although the man who fathered her son never acknowledged him, Rachel gave Matt his surname. As addiction consumed her life, Matt was left wherever she could find someone willing to watch him. When she was home, she was often asleep, lost in her addiction, or prone to violent outbursts. On the rare occasions she reached for her son with genuine affection, Matt had already learned to keep his distance because he never knew whether he would find kindness or violence.
After Rachel's death, Matt was left completely alone to survive. He never knew his father, never knew his maternal grandparents, and believed all his life that his mother had been a Haskins. It was not until years later, before he had eventually met Jake and Luke through Jace and became a family, that someone recognized him as "Rachel Williams' boy." Through that chance encounter, Matt learned his mother's maiden name and discovered, for the first time, the circumstances surrounding her pregnancy, her parents' rejection, and the life she had lived before he was born. By then, there was no one left who could answer the questions he still carried.
Core Beliefs:
She wanted to believe she could quit.
She believed tomorrow would be different.
Her addiction always proved stronger than her promises.
Greatest Strength:
Even beneath her addiction, there were moments when her love for Matt surfaced.
Fatal Flaw:
She never overcame the addiction that consumed her life and stole her ability to be the mother Matt needed.
Psychological Importance:
Rachel taught Matt that love could disappear without warning. As a child, he learned to read voices, expressions, and small changes in behavior because they often signaled whether he was about to receive affection, neglect, or violence. That constant uncertainty shaped the quiet, watchful man he became.
Relationship With Matt:
Matt remembered very little of his mother with any certainty. Most of his childhood was a blur of being left with strangers, waiting for her to return, and never knowing which version of Rachel would walk through the door. On the few occasions she reached for him, he instinctively pulled away because he had learned not to trust what came next.
Locked Thematic Core:
A childhood shaped by addiction, instability, and uncertainty.
Matt Haskins 
Given Name: Matthew Alan Haskins
Born: March 17, 1865
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Strategist • Structural Backbone
Family:
Never married.
Jake Kincaid's closest friend.
Shares a deep, lifelong, completely non-romantic emotional bond with Krys Callahan Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Matt survives by creating order. Planning is not a personality trait—it is how he stays alive. Every decision, every contingency, every calculated risk comes from one belief: if he builds something, he must be able to protect it. If he cannot protect it, he has no right to build it.
Childhood Foundation:
His mother was sixteen when she gave birth to him and struggled with a laudanum addiction her whole life. Matt spent his childhood being passed from one house to another; some treated him kindly, but most didn’t, before finding his mother dead from an overdose at age twelve. From then on, he survived entirely on his own.
Defining Trauma:
At sixteen, he fell deeply in love with Clara Sloan, who was two years older than him. Together they planned a robbery intended only to frighten the store owner into surrendering money so they could marry. Matt misjudged the timing. Clara was shot and died in his arms. He has carried the guilt ever since and has never loved another woman.
Core Formation:
Chaos meant hunger.
Planning meant survival.
Love created risk.
Failure cost blood.
Core Beliefs:
Preparation prevents disaster.

Fatal Flaw:

Touch:
