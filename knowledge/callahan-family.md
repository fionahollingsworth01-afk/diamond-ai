Roger Callahan 
Role: Callahan patriarch • Farmer • Source of family pressure
Family:
Father of Jace, Krys, Rance, Royce, and Rhys Callahan.
Husband of Marie Callahan.
Jace is his son by his first wife. Krys, Rance, Royce, and Rhys are his children with Marie.
Core Spine:
Roger believes family survival depends upon order, obedience, land, reputation, and the authority of the father. He does love his family, but his need to control them often overwhelms his ability to understand them.
Core Formation:
A family must be controlled to remain secure.
Land creates permanence.
Obedience proves respect.
Public reputation protects the family legacy.
Core Beliefs:
The father should lead the household.
Children owe obedience to their parents.
Marriage should improve security, property, and standing.
Emotional closeness can weaken authority.
Family decisions should preserve the Callahan legacy.
Greatest Fear:
Losing authority over his family and watching them reject the future he intended for them.
Greatest Strength:
Persistence, practical knowledge, endurance, and commitment to protecting what belongs to his family.
Fatal Flaw:
Mistakes control for protection and obedience for love.
Psychological Markers:
Has difficulty admitting fault.
Responds poorly when challenged in front of others.
Values land and social respectability heavily.
Often judges people according to usefulness, security, and public standing.
Feels threatened by the equal structure of Five Oaks because no single man rules it.
Relationship With Jace:
Roger never understood Jace’s resistance to authority and treated his eldest son more harshly than the children he had with Marie. Their relationship carries years of rejection, anger, and failed attempts at control.
Relationship With Krys:
Roger loves Krys but repeatedly tries to direct her future. He wanted her to marry Oscar Dunbar because the marriage promised land, security, and respectability. Her refusal represents one of the clearest rejections of his authority.
Relationship With Marie:
Marie often softens the harm Roger causes and protects the children when his need for control goes too far.
Locked Thematic Core:
Control born from fear of disorder and loss.
Marie Callahan 
Role: Callahan matriarch • Emotional protector • Family bridge
Family:
Wife of Roger Callahan.
Mother of Krys, Rance, Royce, and Rhys Callahan.
Stepmother of Jace Callahan, whom she loves and raises as her own.
Later, years after Roger’s death, she marries Five Oak ranch hand Bob Masters
Core Spine:
Marie keeps the family connected even when Roger’s authority threatens to tear it apart. Her strength is quiet but firm, and her love for Jace is never less because she did not give birth to him.
Core Formation:
Children need protection as much as correction.
Love is proven through constancy.
Family bonds are built through care, not blood alone.
Peace is valuable, but not when it requires allowing harm.
Core Beliefs:
Jace is her son in every way that matters.
Children deserve to know they are loved.
Roger’s authority does not excuse cruelty.
Family should remain connected despite conflict.
A mother sometimes has to stand between her children and their father.
Greatest Fear:
Watching Roger permanently drive one or more of their children away.
Greatest Strength:
Patience, emotional endurance, maternal loyalty, and the ability to see pain others hide.
Fatal Flaw:
Endures too much for too long in the hope that peace can still be preserved.
Psychological Markers:
Protective of every Callahan child.
Recognizes Jace’s hurt even when he refuses to speak about it.
Often attempts to reduce conflict before confronting Roger directly.
Carries guilt over times she could not protect the children enough.
Becomes firm when Roger targets Rhys, Jace, or Krys.
Relationship With Jace:
Marie accepts Jace completely and never treats him as less than her biological children. Her love provides one of the few stable maternal bonds in his life.
Relationship With Krys:
Marie understands Krys’s independence better than Roger does, even when she worries about the risks Krys takes.
Relationship With Roger:
Marie loves Roger but increasingly opposes him when his authority becomes harmful.
Locked Thematic Core:
Protection born from enduring love.
Years after Roger’s death she marries a Five Oaks ranch hand named Bob Masters.
Jace Callahan 
Given Name: Jace Michael Callahan
Born: January 9, 1865
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Instigator • Pressure Reader
Family:
Life partner of Susanna Pike.
Father of Eli, Cole, Sawyer, Dawson, Jennifer, and Caleb Callahan.
Core Spine:
Jace challenges pressure wherever he finds it. Authority means nothing if it harms the people he loves. Rules exist to serve family—not the other way around. Once someone becomes his family, his loyalty becomes absolute.
Childhood Foundation:
Born to Roger Callahan's first wife. After her death, Marie became his stepmother and loved him as her own, but Roger never fully accepted him the way he accepted his younger children. Jace learned early that respect could not simply be demanded—it had to be earned.
Core Formation:
Authority creates pressure.
Pressure demands resistance.
Family is never negotiable.
Core Beliefs:
Protect family first.
Earn respect instead of demanding it.
Challenge unfair authority.
Never abandon your own.
Greatest Fear:
Failing to protect the people who matter most.
Greatest Strength:
Fearless loyalty, courage, decisiveness, and protective instinct.
Fatal Flaw:
Pushes against authority so quickly that he sometimes creates conflict before understanding the full situation.
Psychological Markers:
Reads pressure before most people notice it.
Fiercely protective.
Quick to confront injustice.
Speaks directly.
Never abandons family.
Relationship With Krys:
Jace's loyalty to Krys is absolute. Even when they disagree, protecting her remains one of the defining constants of his life.
Locked Thematic Core:
Pressure born from rejection.
Krys Callahan Kincaid 
Given Name: Krysten Irene Callahan Kincaid
Born: April 22, 1869
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Inside Woman • Perception Manipulator
Family:
Wife of Jake Kincaid.
Mother of Kai Weston Kincaid and Wes Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Krys refuses to let others define her life. She understands exactly how people see her and willingly uses those expectations to create opportunities, protect those she loves, and stay one move ahead. She believes belonging should never require surrendering herself.
Background Foundation:
Raised in a respectable farming family alongside her brothers. Learned bookkeeping, rifles, ranch work, and the expectations placed upon women of her time. Rather than submitting to those expectations, she learned how to manipulate them.
Core Formation:
Perception creates opportunity.
Motion creates freedom.
Choice creates identity.
Core Beliefs:
Loyalty must be earned.
Independence and family can coexist.
Intelligence is as powerful as any weapon.
Never surrender your ability to choose.
Greatest Fear:
Losing the balance she has built between family, independence, and purpose.
Greatest Strength:
Adaptability, perception, emotional intelligence, and courage.
Fatal Flaw:
Frequently carries everyone's burdens while pretending she's carrying none.
Psychological Markers:
Reads people almost instantly.
Loyal once committed.
Refuses to become small for anyone.
Buys time through perception and strategy.
Finds belonging deeply important.
Relationship With Matt:
Krys trusts Matt with thoughts she cannot always share with Jake because Matt never panics, never lies simply to comfort her, and never tries to control her decisions.
Locked Thematic Core:
Choice born from expectation.
Rance Callahan 
Given Name: Rance William Callahan
Role: Farmer • Husband • Father•General Store Manager
Family:
Son of Roger and Marie Callahan.
Brother of Jace, Krys, Royce, and Rhys Callahan.
Husband of Erica Callahan.
Father of Ethan, Eric, and Elizabeth Callahan.
Core Spine:
Rance wanted nothing more than to spend his life on Callahan Farm, working beside his family. Years of Roger telling him he wasn't good enough slowly convinced him he would never measure up. Rather than continue living beneath constant criticism, he chose to leave and build a home where his own children would grow up knowing they were enough.
Background Foundation:
Raised on Callahan Farm, Rance genuinely loved farming and expected to spend his life there. As he grew older, Roger became increasingly critical of his work, judgment, and abilities. Instead of building Rance's confidence, Roger slowly destroyed it.
At sixteen, believing he would never satisfy his father, Rance left home and moved in with Erica and her father. One year later, he married Erica. Their first child, Ethan, was born the following year and is the same age as Kai Kincaid.
Core Formation:
Home should build you up, not tear you down.
Hard work deserves respect.
A father should encourage, not belittle.
Love should never depend upon perfection.
Core Beliefs:
Family should feel safe.
Children deserve encouragement.
A person's worth is not measured by another man's approval.
The farm is a home before it is property.
Greatest Fear:
Becoming the kind of father Roger was to him.
Greatest Strength:
Steady work ethic, loyalty, practical farming knowledge, and quiet devotion to his family.
Fatal Flaw:
Years of Roger's criticism left Rance doubting himself long after he had proven he was capable. Even as an adult, he sometimes questions his own judgment before trusting it.
Psychological Markers:
Works hard without seeking recognition.
Avoids unnecessary conflict.
Still carries emotional scars from Roger's constant criticism.
Protective of Erica and their children.
Finds peace through farming rather than through confrontation.
Relationship With Roger:
Rance never stopped loving his father, but Roger's relentless criticism convinced him he would never be good enough. Leaving Callahan Farm was an act of survival rather than rebellion.
Relationship With Erica:
Erica and her father gave Rance the acceptance and encouragement he had long been missing. He gave him a job as a clerk in his General Store and let him work his way up to manager. Their home became the foundation upon which he rebuilt his confidence. 
Relationship With Krys:
Krys understands better than most how deeply Roger's words affected Rance. She never saw him as weak, only wounded by expectations he could never satisfy.
Locked Thematic Core:
Self-worth reclaimed after years of believing he wasn't enough.
Royce Callahan 
Given Name: Royce Daniel Callahan
Role: Callahan sibling • Former Outlaw • Former Prisoner
Family:
Son of Roger and Marie Callahan.
Brother of Jace, Krys, Rance, and Rhys Callahan.
Never married.
Core Spine:
Royce spent his life searching for a place where he belonged. When Roger's constant criticism convinced him he would never earn approval at home, he looked elsewhere. The outlaw life offered acceptance without judgment, but it ultimately cost him twenty-five years of freedom. Even after prison, his greatest hope remains finding his way back to the family he never truly stopped loving.
Background Foundation:
Royce grew up on Callahan Farm but struggled beneath Roger's expectations. By fourteen, he had begun spending time in town with rough company, finding acceptance among boys who expected nothing from him except loyalty. Roger responded with increasing pressure, criticism, and attempts to force Royce back into line. Instead of changing him, it pushed him farther away.
At sixteen, Royce left home and joined an outlaw gang, believing he had no place left at Callahan Farm.
Life History:
Royce rode with an outlaw gang until a bank robbery ended in disaster. Most of the gang was killed during the attempted robbery.
With nowhere else to go, Royce sought refuge at Five Oaks. His brothers and sister gave him shelter without judgment. When he learned lawmen had begun asking questions in town, he quietly left rather than risk bringing danger to Five Oaks or the people living there.
Some time later he was captured in Oklahoma Territory by a United States Territorial Marshal. He was convicted and spent the next twenty-five years in prison.
Core Formation:
Acceptance is easy to mistake for belonging.
A person can choose the wrong family while searching for the right one.
Some mistakes cannot be undone.
Love can survive decades of absence.
Core Beliefs:
Family deserves protection, even from your own mistakes.
Freedom is precious because it can disappear overnight.
The past cannot be changed, but it can be faced.
Belonging is worth fighting for.
Greatest Fear:
Returning home only to discover there is no place left for him within his family.
Greatest Strength:
Extraordinary endurance, resilience, and the ability to survive hardship without losing his humanity.
Fatal Flaw:
Spent years believing he had already failed beyond redemption, making it difficult to believe he deserved forgiveness even after prison.
Psychological Markers:
Carries deep regret without seeking sympathy.
Protective of his family despite believing he forfeited the right to be part of it.
Accepts responsibility for his choices.
Quietly observant after years of confinement.
Finds ordinary freedom unfamiliar after decades behind bars.
Relationship With Roger:
Roger's relentless criticism and attempts to control him helped drive Royce toward the very people Roger feared. Royce's rebellion grew from believing he would never be enough in his father's eyes.
Relationship With Marie:
Marie never stopped loving Royce or hoping he would someday come home.
Relationship With His Siblings:
Royce never stopped considering the Callahans his family. Seeking refuge at Five Oaks before his capture demonstrated that, when everything else was gone, he still believed his brothers and sister would take him in.
Five Oaks:
Royce willingly left Five Oaks after learning that law enforcement had begun asking questions because he refused to bring danger upon the ranch or the family that had sheltered him.
Locked Thematic Core:
Belonging sought in the wrong place, then fought for the rest of his life.
Rhys Callahan 
Role: Youngest Callahan sibling • Husband • Father
Family:
Youngest son of Roger and Marie Callahan.
Brother of Jace, Krys, Rance, and Royce Callahan.
Husband of Olivia Collins Callahan.
Father of Emily and Randall Rhyleigh Callahan.
Core Spine:
Rhys longs to be respected within a family where he was often treated as the child, especially by Roger.
Core Beliefs:
He deserves to be treated as a man.
Family loyalty does not require accepting humiliation.
A home should not be ruled through fear.
Olivia and their children deserve better than the childhood patterns he knew.
Greatest Fear:
Becoming powerless under Roger’s authority again.
Greatest Strength:
Loyalty, emotional openness, and the ability to build a gentler family life.
Fatal Flaw:
Old fear of Roger can make him retreat before defending himself.
Major Family Event:
Roger strikes Rhys during the month-long visit to Five Oaks. Jace later intervenes when Roger appears ready to strike him again.
Rhys and Roger have an argument, after which Rhys leaves the next morning. Roger waits to try and talk to him. Rhys brushes past him. Later that morning, Roger dies of a massive heart attack while at work. The last words he ever spoke to Rhys was his name, leaving him feeling guilty for a long time afterwards.
Locked Thematic Core:
Adulthood claimed after years of being diminished.
