Krys Callahan Kincaid 
Given Name: Krysten Irene Callahan Kincaid
Born: April 22, 1869
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Inside Woman • Perception Manipulator
Family:
Wife of Jake Kincaid.
Mother of Kai Weston Kincaid and Wes Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Krys refuses to let others define her life. She understands exactly how people see her and willingly uses those expectations to create opportunities, protect those she loves, and stay one move ahead. She believes belonging should never require surrendering herself.
Background Foundation:
Raised in a respectable farming family alongside her brothers. Learned bookkeeping, rifles, ranch work, and the expectations placed upon women of her time. Rather than submitting to those expectations, she learned how to manipulate them.
Core Formation:
Perception creates opportunity.
Motion creates freedom.
Choice creates identity.
Core Beliefs:
Loyalty must be earned.
Independence and family can coexist.
Intelligence is as powerful as any weapon.
Never surrender your ability to choose.
Greatest Fear:
Losing the balance she has built between family, independence, and purpose.
Greatest Strength:
Adaptability, perception, emotional intelligence, and courage.
Fatal Flaw:
Frequently carries everyone's burdens while pretending she's carrying none.
Psychological Markers:
Reads people almost instantly.
Loyal once committed.
Refuses to become small for anyone.
Buys time through perception and strategy.
Finds belonging deeply important.
Relationship With Matt:
Krys trusts Matt with thoughts she cannot always share with Jake because Matt never panics, never lies simply to comfort her, and never tries to control her decisions.
Locked Thematic Core:
Choice born from expectation.
Jake Kincaid  
Given Name: Jacob Weston Kincaid
Born: November 3, 1866
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Enforcer • Anchor
Family:
Husband of Krys Callahan Kincaid.
Father of Kai Weston Kincaid and Wes Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Jake protects. Everything else is secondary. His entire life is built around creating a home where the people he loves never have to live in fear again. Silence became his armor after years of abuse, and violence became a tool to end danger—not prolong it. He believes loyalty is absolute once earned, cruelty is weakness, and suffering is easier to carry alone than to place on someone else's shoulders.
Childhood Foundation:
Jake grew up with an absent father, an emotionally distant mother, an abusive stepfather, and abusive stepbrothers. His mother never intervened. At thirteen, he chose hunger over another day in that house and left home to survive on his own.
Core Formation:
Pain lasts longer when you react.
Silence is armor.
End conflict quickly before it grows.
Core Beliefs:
Loyalty is absolute once earned.
Violence must be clean and necessary.
Cruelty is weakness.
Family always comes first.
A man protects; he never intimidates.
Greatest Fear:
Becoming the kind of man who filled his childhood home with fear, especially in the eyes of his own children.
Greatest Strength:
Unshakable loyalty, emotional steadiness, and quiet leadership.
Fatal Flaw:
Carries every burden himself and rarely allows others to share his pain.
Psychological Markers:
Speaks only when necessary.
Rarely wastes movement.
Ends fights rather than escalating them.
Never enjoys violence.
Constantly measures himself against the men who abused him.
Locked Thematic Core:
Finality born from abuse and silence.
Kai Kincaid 
Role: Eldest son of Jake and Krys • Older brother • Husband • Five Oaks heir
Family:
Eldest son of Jake Kincaid and Krys Callahan Kincaid.
Older brother of Wes Kincaid.
Husband of Paloma Echevarría.
Core Spine:
Kai carries the legacy of Five Oaks while trying to build a life that belongs to him rather than merely repeating his parents’ story.
Core Formation:
Family is a source of strength and responsibility.
A legacy must be carried forward, not preserved unchanged.
Love can arrive after a person has stopped expecting it.
Home expands when new people are welcomed into it.
Core Beliefs:
Five Oaks belongs to the future as much as the past.
His parents’ sacrifices deserve respect.
Paloma is his chosen family.
He has a responsibility to help hold the next generation together.
Greatest Fear:
Failing the family legacy entrusted to him.
Greatest Strength:
Loyalty, patience, steadiness, and willingness to accept new possibilities.
Fatal Flaw:
Can place too much responsibility upon himself because he is Jake and Krys’s eldest son.
Psychological Markers:
Deeply attached to Five Oaks.
Respects Jake’s quiet strength and Krys’s independence.
Had largely given up on finding love before Paloma’s family arrived.
Protective of Wes without trying to control him.
Understands that family can grow through choice.
Relationship With Paloma:
Paloma gives Kai a future he had stopped expecting. Their marriage represents the widening world of Five Oaks.
Locked Thematic Core:
Legacy carried into a future he chooses.
Wesley Jon Kincaid 
Role: Younger son of Jake and Krys • Husband • Father • Five Oaks heir
Family:
Youngest son of Jake Kincaid and Krys Callahan Kincaid.
Younger brother of Kai Kincaid.
Husband of Samantha “Sami” Rawlins Kincaid.
Father of Wesley Lucas Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Wes goes to the School of Business and Agriculture in Springfield for four years. When he graduates, he returns home with what he learned and builds his place within Five Oaks through family, partnership, and the life he creates with Sami rather than through comparison with Kai.
Core Formation:
Being the younger son does not mean living in his brother’s shadow.
Marriage is a partnership.
Family traditions should support life rather than restrict it.
Fatherhood offers the chance to carry forward the best of Jake and Krys.
Core Beliefs:
Sami is his equal.
His son deserves a secure and loving home.
Kai’s role does not lessen his own.
Five Oaks belongs to all who help preserve it.
Greatest Fear:
Failing Sami or their son when they depend upon him.
Greatest Strength:
Loyalty, warmth, dependability, and devotion to family.
Fatal Flaw:
May underestimate his own importance beside stronger or louder family figures.
Psychological Markers:
Protective of Sami.
Close to Kai despite their differences.
Carries pieces of both Jake’s steadiness and Krys’s independence.
Values domestic happiness without seeing it as weakness.
Locked Thematic Core:
Identity built beside legacy, not beneath it.
Paloma Echevarría Kincaid
Role: Kai's wife • Daughter • Member of the Five Oaks family
Family:
Wife of Kai Weston Kincaid.
Daughter of Mateo and Catalina Echevarría.
Older sister of Isabella Echevarría.
Daughter-in-law of Jake and Krys Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Paloma chooses love without surrendering who she is. She embraces both her Spanish heritage and her new life at Five Oaks, believing that family grows stronger when different traditions are welcomed instead of erased.
Background Foundation:
Raised in a loving Spanish household surrounded by strong family traditions, respect, and deep affection. Learned confidence, kindness, and independence from her parents.
Core Formation:
Love requires trust.
Family can grow without losing its roots.
Kindness creates belonging.
Joy deserves to be shared.
Core Beliefs:
Marriage is a partnership.
Family should welcome rather than exclude.
Traditions deserve to be honored.
Love grows through everyday life.
Greatest Fear:
Losing the closeness of either family she loves.
Greatest Strength:
Warmth, optimism, emotional intelligence, and unwavering devotion.
Fatal Flaw:
Can underestimate how deeply others worry because she naturally expects the best from people.
Psychological Markers:
Naturally affectionate.
Laughs easily.
Curious about others.
Builds relationships quickly.
Adapts without losing her identity.
Relationship With Kai:
Paloma becomes the answer to the future Kai had quietly stopped believing would ever happen. Their relationship is built on friendship, mutual respect, trust, and genuine happiness rather than drama.
Relationship With Krys:
Krys welcomes Paloma almost immediately, treating her like another daughter. Paloma responds with deep affection and quickly becomes part of the heart of Five Oaks.
Locked Thematic Core:
Belonging born from love and acceptance.

Jace Callahan 
Given Name: Jace Michael Callahan
Born: January 9, 1865
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Instigator • Pressure Reader
Family:
Life partner of Susanna Pike.
Father of Eli, Cole, Sawyer, Dawson, Jennifer, and Caleb Callahan.
Core Spine:
Jace challenges pressure wherever he finds it. Authority means nothing if it harms the people he loves. Rules exist to serve family—not the other way around. Once someone becomes his family, his loyalty becomes absolute.
Childhood Foundation:
Born to Roger Callahan's first wife. After her death, Marie became his stepmother and loved him as her own, but Roger never fully accepted him the way he accepted his younger children. Jace learned early that respect could not simply be demanded—it had to be earned.
Core Formation:
Authority creates pressure.
Pressure demands resistance.
Family is never negotiable.
Core Beliefs:
Protect family first.
Earn respect instead of demanding it.
Challenge unfair authority.
Never abandon your own.
Greatest Fear:
Failing to protect the people who matter most.
Greatest Strength:
Fearless loyalty, courage, decisiveness, and protective instinct.
Fatal Flaw:
Pushes against authority so quickly that he sometimes creates conflict before understanding the full situation.
Psychological Markers:
Reads pressure before most people notice it.
Fiercely protective.
Quick to confront injustice.
Speaks directly.
Never abandons family.
Relationship With Krys:
Jace's loyalty to Krys is absolute. Even when they disagree, protecting her remains one of the defining constants of his life.
Locked Thematic Core:
Pressure born from rejection.

Matt Haskins 
Given Name: Matthew Alan Haskins
Born: March 17, 1865
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Strategist • Structural Backbone
Family:
Never married.
Jake Kincaid's closest friend.
Shares a deep, lifelong, completely non-romantic emotional bond with Krys Callahan Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Matt survives by creating order. Planning is not a personality trait—it is how he stays alive. Every decision, every contingency, every calculated risk comes from one belief: if he builds something, he must be able to protect it. If he cannot protect it, he has no right to build it.
Childhood Foundation:
His mother was sixteen when she gave birth to him and struggled with laudanum addiction. Matt spent his childhood being passed from one house to another before finding his mother dead from an overdose at age twelve. From then on he survived entirely on his own.
Defining Trauma:
At sixteen he fell deeply in love with Clara Sloan. Together they planned a robbery intended only to frighten the store owner into surrendering money so they could marry. Matt misjudged the timing. Clara was shot and died in his arms. He has carried the guilt ever since and has never loved another woman.
Core Formation:
Chaos meant hunger.
Planning meant survival.
Love created risk.
Failure cost blood.
Core Beliefs:
Preparation prevents disaster.
Responsibility never ends.
Stability matters more than comfort.
Love carries unbearable risk.
He does not deserve forgiveness.
Greatest Fear:
Watching someone else die because of his failure.
Greatest Strength:
Extraordinary planning, observation, patience, and loyalty.
Fatal Flaw:
Believes he is fundamentally unsafe to love and carries responsibility for everything that goes wrong.
Touch:
Matt dislikes physical affection from women because intimacy represents attachment, responsibility, and eventual loss. Krys is the lone exception because she steadies him without demanding anything from him.
Psychological Markers:
Notices details others miss.
Sleeps the least.
Rarely shows pain.
Never abandons someone mid-job.
Carries guilt as a permanent part of himself.
Locked Thematic Core:
Control born from chaos and guilt.
Luke Rawlins 
Given Name: Lucas Paul Rawlins
Born: August 11, 1868
Role: Founder of Five Oaks • Charmer • Distraction
Family:
Husband of Emma Richards Rawlins.
Father of Samantha "Sami" Rawlins Kincaid.
Core Spine:
Luke protects people by carrying the attention himself. Humor, charm, and easy conversation became shields long before they became personality. If people are watching him, they are not hurting someone else.
Childhood Foundation:
Luke grew up with a violent alcoholic father who regularly abused his mother. He also had two younger sisters, Astrid and Iris, whom he constantly tried to protect by redirecting his father's attention toward himself. He finally left home at fourteen after a beating that convinced him staying would eventually get someone killed.
Core Formation:
Charm redirects violence.
Staying creates targets.
Leave before attachment becomes leverage.
Core Beliefs:
Protect children at all costs.
Ease tension whenever possible.
Laughter keeps despair from taking over.
Better to carry the burden yourself.
Greatest Fear:
Someone he loves becomes a victim because of him.
Greatest Strength:
Empathy, adaptability, emotional intelligence, and optimism.
Fatal Flaw:
Uses humor to avoid confronting painful emotions and sometimes manipulates situations believing he's helping.
Psychological Markers:
Hyperaware of changing moods.
Deflects tension naturally.
Uses humor to calm others.
Reads rooms almost instantly.
Cannot tolerate abuse toward children.
Widow Incident:
Luke genuinely believed arranging companionship for Matt would help him heal. Only later did he realize he had unintentionally taken away Matt's sense of control, and the realization filled him with genuine guilt.
Locked Thematic Core:
Charm born from violence and protection.
