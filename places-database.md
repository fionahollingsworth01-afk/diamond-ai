# FIVE OAKS PLACES DATABASE

This file is a canon reference for important places in the Five Oaks saga. When details here conflict with assumptions, use this file and the manuscripts as authority.

## Five Oaks Ranch

**Location:** Near Red Willow Crossing, Missouri.

**Role:** Central home of the saga and the ranch built by Jake Kincaid, Krys Callahan Kincaid, Matt Haskins, Luke Rawlins, and Jace Callahan.

**Ownership:** The five founders hold equal shares.

**Primary Features:**
- Main house where the family gathers for meals.
- Founders' cabins.
- Barn and loft.
- Corrals and pastures.
- Creek.
- Cornfield fenced away from cattle.
- Hog and chicken areas.
- Later cabins for the next generation.
- Eli's cabin and small barn.
- Kai and Paloma's cabin on the north path.
- Wes and Sami's cabin farther up the path behind the main cabin.

**Importance:** Five Oaks is more than property. It becomes a chosen-family home where protection, shared work, and belonging matter more than blood.

---

## Red Willow Crossing, Missouri

**Role:** Primary nearby town serving Five Oaks.

**Known Places Within Town:**
- Bank.
- Livery.
- Feed store.
- General store.
- Saloon.
- Boarding house.
- Hotel.
- Railroad depot.
- Patel's Eatery.
- The Belle Époque French restaurant.
- Doctor's office and hospital.
- Sheriff's office.
- Main Street.

**Importance:** The town is the center of trade, law, medical care, gossip, conflict, and changing public opinion surrounding Five Oaks.

---

## Callahan Farm

**Role:** Childhood home of Jace, Krys, Rance, Royce, and Rhys Callahan.

**Family:** Roger and Marie Callahan's farm.

**Importance:** Rance loved the farm and expected to remain there, but Roger's criticism drove him away. Royce was also pushed away by Roger's attempts to control him. The farm represents inheritance, expectation, and family pressure.

---

## Johnson Farm

**Owner:** Albert "Old Man" Johnson.

**Role:** Early workplace of Jace, Jake, Matt, and Luke.

**Importance:** Johnson pays poorly and works the men relentlessly. His treatment convinces them they need another way to survive and helps set the founders' shared future in motion.

---

## Milliron Farm

**Owners:** Henry and Martha Milliron.

**Location:** Near Dover.

**Role:** Temporary home and workplace for Jake, Matt, Luke, and Krys after they leave Johnson's farm.

**Importance:** The Millirons give them honest work, food, shelter, and respect. Martha becomes an early maternal influence on Krys.

---

## High Willow Ranch

**Owner:** Silas Vance.

**Role:** Wealthy neighboring ranch and source of conflict over the founders' creek property.

**Importance:** Silas Vance wants the founders' land because of its long-term value and uses agents, threats, and hired men to pressure them.

---

## Richards Farm

**Owners:** Henry and Eleanor Richards.

**Family Connection:** Childhood home of Emma Richards Rawlins and her brothers Thomas and Samuel.

**Importance:** Represents Emma's stable family background and remains connected to Five Oaks through her marriage to Luke.

---

## Pike Home

**Family:** Alva Pike and daughters Susanna, Beatrice "Bea," and Rosalie "Rose."

**Importance:** Susanna's family home before she builds a life with Jace.

---

## Patterson Property / Patterson Farm

**Role:** Old farm near Five Oaks that later becomes central to Sawyer and Dawson's oil venture.

**Known Features:**
- Old farmhouse and outbuildings.
- Restored outbuildings used for workers.
- Land chosen for oil drilling.
- Near the creek and the route toward Waya and Tsula's area.

**Importance:** The Patterson property moves from neglected farmland into the center of the next generation's future. Fred Ward and his sons live in one of its restored outbuildings while Fred works on the rig.

---

## Oil Well Site

**Location:** Patterson property.

**Operators:** Sawyer and Dawson Callahan.

**Role:** Drilling site for the twins' oil venture.

**Importance:** Represents the next generation creating something new instead of only inheriting the ranch.

---

## Creek on Five Oaks Property

**Role:** Important natural boundary and meeting place.

**Importance:** Used by the family over the years and later tied to Jennifer, Waya, Tsula, and the Patterson side of the ranch map.

---

## Ridge Overlooking Five Oaks

**Role:** Hidden observation point above the ranch.

**Importance:** Eli watches Five Oaks from the ridge while separated from his family. It represents longing, distance, protection, and his fear of returning home.

---

## Waya and Tsula's Hillside Camp

**Location:** Beyond the Patterson property and not on Five Oaks land.

**Role:** Home area connected to Waya, Tsula, Tavi, and their Cherokee family.

**Importance:** Becomes central to Jennifer's growing relationship with Waya and Tsula.

---

## Cherokee Community Near Red Willow Crossing

**Role:** Home community of Waya Red Hawk, Tsula, Galilahi, Salali, Tavi Gray Feather, Aiyana Gray Feather, and their extended families.

**Importance:** Carries family, cultural identity, tradition, and community ties into Jennifer and Waya's story.

---

## Manchester, Oklahoma

**Known Places:**
- Miss Maxine's boarding house.
- Patel's Eatery in the broader story world is in Red Willow Crossing, not Manchester.
- Saloon.
- Streets and alleys used during Eli's outlaw years.

**Importance:** Eli spends time there while trying to survive after leaving the gang. Miss Maxine notices his injuries and quietly watches over him.

---

## Redstone, Oklahoma

**Role:** Location of Ironjaw Winston's hideout.

**Known Feature:** A rough shack built into a hillside.

**Importance:** Eli leaves from Ironjaw's camp and begins his ride home to Missouri.

---

## Cedar Gap, Missouri

**Role:** Railroad connection point.

**Importance:** Part of Whisper Winston's delayed trip toward Red Willow Crossing.

---

## Dover

**Role:** Town or area near the Milliron farm.

**Importance:** Connected to the period when the founders work for Henry and Martha Milliron.

---

## Springfield

**Role:** Education center.

**Importance:** Wes attends the School of Business and Agriculture there for four years before returning to Five Oaks.

---

## St. Louis

**Role:** Major city used for travel, business, medical care, shopping, and family visits.

**Known Connections:**
- Luke's Overland Touring automobile is ordered from a dealership there.
- Families travel there for visits and appointments.
- Goods and fashions reach Red Willow Crossing from St. Louis.

---

## Five Oaks Main House

**Role:** Shared family center.

**Known Uses:**
- Communal meals.
- Family meetings.
- Medical recovery.
- Weddings and celebrations.
- Guest lodging when needed.

**Importance:** The main house represents the founders' decision to live as one extended family rather than five separate households.

---

## Five Oaks Barn and Loft

**Role:** Working barn with an upper loft.

**Known Uses:**
- Ranch work and horse care.
- Temporary sleeping space.
- Cole and Tate stay in the loft before receiving their own place.
- Micah Ward hides there for days before Krys finds him.

**Importance:** The loft repeatedly becomes a place of concealment, vulnerability, and eventual belonging.

---

## Krys and Jake's Cabin

**Residents:** Jake Kincaid and Krys Callahan Kincaid.

**Known Use:** Micah Ward sleeps on the couch after Krys takes him in because Cole and Tate still occupy the loft.

**Importance:** Becomes Micah's first safe shelter after fleeing Fred Ward.

---

## Eli's Cabin

**Resident:** Eli Callahan.

**Importance:** Built after Eli returns, giving him a permanent place at Five Oaks and physical proof that the family expects him to stay.

---

## Kai and Paloma's Cabin

**Location:** North path at Five Oaks.

**Importance:** Represents Kai building a future of his own while remaining tied to the ranch.

---

## Wes and Sami's Cabin

**Location:** Up the path behind the main cabin.

**Importance:** Home of Wes, Sami, and Wesley Lucas Kincaid.

---

## Miss Maxine's Boarding House

**Location:** Manchester, Oklahoma.

**Owner:** Miss Maxine.

**Importance:** A place where Eli receives quiet concern without interrogation during his attempt to survive away from Whisper.

---

## The Belle Époque

**Type:** French restaurant.

**Location:** Red Willow Crossing.

**Importance:** Site of major family meals, including the dinner where Kai and the Kincaids spend time with Mateo, Catalina, Paloma, and Isabella Echevarría.

---

## Patel's Eatery

**Location:** Red Willow Crossing.

**Owner:** Patel.

**Importance:** Longstanding local restaurant known for dependable food and familiarity. Members of Five Oaks have eaten there since childhood.

---

## Red Willow Crossing Hospital

**Role:** Medical center.

**Importance:** Krys undergoes surgery and recovers there after Whisper shoots her. The family keeps vigil in its halls.

---

## Bank of Red Willow Crossing

**Role:** Employer, business center, and site connected to earlier robberies and later financial dealings.

**Importance:** Krys's banking knowledge and access shape key events in the founders' early years.

---

## Railroad Depot

**Location:** Red Willow Crossing.

**Role:** Passenger and freight connection.

**Importance:** Brings automobiles, goods, travelers, and information into town and supports Conrad Hensley's business dealings.
