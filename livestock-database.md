LIVESTOCK DATABASE

Hellbender 

The first great herd bull at Five Oaks. He was red and white, gentle enough to let Kai climb all over him, and unusually good-natured. The family considered him the standard every bull afterward was measured against. He was supposedly afraid of frogs, though Jake denied it. Hellbender was later killed.
A massive Shorthorn bull.
Type: Herd Bull
Breed: Shorthorn
Color: Red and White
Temperament: Gentle, calm, dependable
History:
The original legendary Five Oaks herd bull. Loved by the founders and trusted around the children. Set the standard every later herd bull was measured against.

Ironhead 

A big red Hereford bull Matt named. They bought him after Hellbender died. He was mean as hell, aggressive with both cattle and people, and once charged Rhys hard enough to slam the fence while Rhys barely escaped.
Type: Herd Bull
Temperament: Aggressive, dominant, unpredictable
History:
Purchased after Hellbender. Frequently challenged fences and people. One of the most dangerous bulls Five Oaks ever owned.

Stonewall 

A broad, solid, calm, and old by the later books. He never wasted movement and refused to give Ironhead the fight Ironhead wanted, which aggravated Ironhead even more. Bringing Stonewall into the herd helped manage the trouble Ironhead caused.
Type: Herd Bull
Temperament: Calm, steady, mature
History:
An older experienced bull brought in while Ironhead was still part of the herd. His quiet confidence contrasted sharply with Ironhead's aggression.

Buckskin

The newer pale cream bull. He was broad through the chest, observant, calm, and approached the fence without charging or showing off. The breeder described him as one of the calmest bulls he had handled, and Matt thought Buckskin reminded him of Hellbender.
Type: Herd Bull
Color: Light cream (buckskin-colored)

Temperament: Calm, observant
History:
Purchased after the founders were impressed with his disposition. Matt immediately noticed similarities to Hellbender.

Buttermilk

Five Oaks Tradition

As the founders’ children grew up, they were occasionally allowed to name calves born on the ranch. Those names became part of Five Oaks history because they represented the next generation beginning to leave its own mark on the ranch.
Sami named Biscuit when she was little.
Jennifer named Cornbread and Buttermilk when they were young twins.
